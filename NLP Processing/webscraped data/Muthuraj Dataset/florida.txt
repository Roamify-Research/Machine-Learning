2. Miami

Image Source
Located in southern Florida, Miami is one of the most beautiful cities in the state. Miami and neighboring Miami Beach are totally distinct. The city is modern that acutely contrasts with a relaxed atmosphere over Miami Beach. South Beach is the most attractive beach in Miami, famous for its Art Deco district and a very trendy pedestrian area. The beach is full of outdoor restaurants imparting sights of seas. Flagler Street in downtown Miami has many historic attractions and Brickell and Design District has many luxury shops and elegant restaurants. The Caribbean influence is found here, and the Overtown area is known for its rich African American history and delicious food. The Vizcaya Museum and the historic stone houses amidst Coconut Grove are worth exploring and staying at.

3. Tampa

Image Source
Tampa’s Ybor City is one of the most historic places where food is a must in the famous Columbia restaurant. Tampa is very popular for its wildlife attractions. Busch Gardens is the most famous giant amusement park connected to the zoo, where there are many things to see and do. Animal lovers can visit the Tampa Zoo at Lowry Park and the Tampa Aquarium, as well as a public observation center with a manatee. Tourists can also visit Big Cat Rescue, a sanctuary for abused and abandoned cats, including lions, tigers, jaguars and more. Families with younger children will love to spend the time in Glazer Children’s Museum. The museum is great for learning through creative fun, and the whole family will enjoy it all together. The Museum of Science and Industry lets you explore the world of technology with live shows at the IMAX Theater.

Destin is a city in northwest Florida, on a peninsula that separates the Gulf of Mexico and the Gulf of Choctawhatchee. Destin is famous for its beautiful beaches with fine white sand, many wonderful golf courses, and fantastic fishing. You can fish a lot here. Fish eaters are really going to enjoy the meal over here. Feel the city walking along the Destin Harbor waterfront. Head to Henderson Beach State Park to see the dense dunes and the trees. If you are carrying younger ones along with you, they will definitely love the variety of slides, pools, and waterfalls in the huge Big Kahuna’s water park. Visit the Destin History and Fishing Museum to learn about the importance of fishing for Destin. The Fort Walton is an archaeological site, built by the Pensacola tribe in 850 A.D. The fort is beautiful to explore.

9. Sanibel Island

Image Source
Sanibel Island is located off the west coast of Florida as an important barrier island, connected to Fort Myers via a dike. Sanibel is famous for its luxury resorts that are popular with families, celebrities and couples. In addition to generous amenities in every resort, tourists will not miss activities, from adventures at sea to daily shopping in boutiques and galleries of Sanibel. One of the most interesting aspects of Sanibel and Captiva is devotion to conservation, a mission which means protecting the condition of the peninsula and creating organizations such as the Sanibel Sea School and the Sanibel-Captiva Conservation Foundation. Many resorts also have their own programs run by naturalists to educate tourists about the delicate ecosystems and stunning nature. There are plenty of turtles, manatees and hundreds of bird species and dolphins play in nearby waters.

The southernmost city along the Florida Gulf Coast is Naples, an exclusive area with an ancient historic center and beautiful beach sections. A mixture of elegant houses and high condominium towers stands along the ocean, but the city center still maintains the atmosphere of a small town. Napoli ‘Third St. South and 5th Avenue South is home to the most fashionable restaurants, art galleries, and boutiques. A visit to the Naples pier, the city’s most loved landmark, is a must for tourists and an ideal place to see dolphins without riding a boat. In addition to the reputation of exclusive golf resorts, Naples has several historic sights and unique natural parks. The Everglades Visitor Center on the Gulf Coast is the only free access point to the national park and visitors can take a boat trip or rent a kayak or visit the mangrove river and see the extraordinary nature of the region.

Frequently Asked Questions About Places To Visit In Florida

What is Florida is famous for?

						Florida is well-known for its theme parks and exotic beaches which attracts millions every year and still surprises many travelers with its beauty.

						Florida as in whole is cool to live in. However, to name the best ones, these are the some of the best cities to live in – Pensacola, Tampa, Orlando.

What’s the most beautiful place in Florida?

						Although it is difficult to point out one beautiful place in Florida, Sanibel Island is most of the times said to be one of the most beautiful places to explore here.

What do you need to know about Florida before visiting?

						Florida has more toll roads and bridges than any other state in the Union.

What food is Florida famous for?

						Some of the most famous foods in Florida are Florida Orange Juice, Key lime pie, The Cuban Sandwich, Conch Fritters, Florida Stone Crab, Strawberries/Strawberry Shortcake, Craft Beer and Apalachicola Oysters.

What is the cheapest city in Florida to live in?

						Palm Coast and Orlando are said to be the cheapest cities to live in Florida. So, if you are on a tight budget, you might want to consider one of these cities.

What is the best time to visit Florida?

						The best time to visit Florida is From April to May and September to November as the temperature dips down a bit, making it a beautiful weather for sightseeing.
